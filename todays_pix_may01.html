May 2 is Free Comic Book Day, which means comic shops across the country are handing out free issues to anyone who walks in. To mark the occasion, we've rounded up the best comic book movies, graphic novels, and TV shows worth your weekend.

 

Panel to Picture: The Best Comic Book Movies

Comic book movies have come a long way since the early days of stiff capes and corny dialogue. These adaptations earned their fans by treating the source material with care.

Spider-Man 2

Twenty-two years later, Sam Raimi's 2004 sequel still sets the bar for superhero movies. Alfred Molina's Doc Ock is one of the great comic book villain performances, and the hospital scene is simply perfection. The train fight alone earns the rewatch.

The Marylebone

The Vibe: Trendy with a twist

The Location: London, UK

Instaworthy Accommodations:

Marylebone Village is one of London's trendiest neighborhoods, so, naturally, the hotel named for it (and located in it) is just as chic. Elegant without being stodgy, and trendy without being uncomfortable, The Marylebone hits the sweet spot between style and substance. Rooms feature understated furnishings, playful accents, and crisp linens. Plus, guests get complimentary access to on-site Third Space, an exclusive health club with an 18m ozone-treated pool. When hunger strikes, dine at the charming 108 Brasserie and check out their top-tier gin selection! Pro tip: nab a terrace suite for a private, all-weather terrace with TV and fireplace.

Required Reading: Graphic Novels Worth the Shelf Space

On Free Comic Book Day, you can grab a free issue, but if you want to dive deeper into the lore, these graphic novels deserve a spot on your TBR.

Watchmen

Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons' 1986 superhero deconstruction was the first comic to ever win a Hugo Award. Its signature nine-panel is taught in art schools, and forty years later, you can still feel its DNA in new graphic novels.

Hotel Spotlight

Together with Inside Hotels

Broadwick Soho

The Vibe: Decadent, daring, and distinctly Soho

The Location: London, England

Instaworthy Accommodations:

Equal parts glamorous and grounded, Broadwick Soho is a 57-room retreat inspired by the grit, charm, and creative chaos of its neighborhood. Dreamed up by a group of friends, the hotel is filled with personality, layered with plush textures, bespoke furniture, curated artwork, and just the right amount of elegance. Start your day with coffee at Bar Jackie, unwind by the fireplace in The Nook, indulge in Italian classics at Dear Jackie, and end the night at Flute, the rooftop bar with skyline views and an after-hours vibe. At Broadwick Soho, London’s historic charm meets unapologetic luxury.

Pulled from the Page: The Best Comic Book TV Shows

Comics run on cliffhangers and slow-burn arcs, which makes them a more natural fit for TV than film. These series use the extra runway to land emotional payoffs that stick with viewers long after the credits roll.

Invincible

Robert Kirkman's animated series follows a teenage superhero learning his all-powerful father might not be the hero everyone thought. Four seasons in, the animation has been criticized (rightfully so), but the incredible writing keeps you invested in characters voiced by Steven Yeun, J.K. Simmons, and Sandra Oh.
