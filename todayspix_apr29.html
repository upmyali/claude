Hey, friend!

International Jazz Day is April 30, and you don’t have to own a record player or know the difference between bebop and hard bop to enjoy it. Here are the movies, books, and shows that capture the true essence of jazz music.

 

10 Films That Get Jazz Right

Most films about jazz get the vibes but miss the music. These ten understand both. Queue one up, preferably after dark, and preferably with a good set of speakers.

Mo’ Better Blues

Spike Lee’s 1990 drama follows Denzel Washington as Bleek Gilliam, a trumpeter trying to hold his band and his life together. Terence Blanchard played the trumpet behind Denzel’s performance, Branford Marsalis led the quartet, and Bill Lee wrote the score. The result sounds like the real thing because it is.

After Hours — 10 Essential Jazz Books

As one of, if not the only, art form America can honestly claim as its own, jazz music deserves context. These ten books dig into the lives and stories behind the music.

But Beautiful: A Book About Jazz
by Geoff Dyers

Geoff Dyer’s 1991 book imagines scenes from the lives of eight jazz legends, Lester Young to Thelonious Monk, in prose that reads like the music itself. It’s the only book about jazz that jazz musicians seem to recommend without reservation.

Soulful Jazz Shows That Make You Want to Groove

Jazz on TV is rarer than it should be. These shows respect the genre, offering incredible soundtracks. Turn up the volume and settle in.

Treme

David Simon’s follow-up to The Wire spent four seasons inside post-Katrina New Orleans, and the city’s music is built into every frame. Real jazz and brass musicians play themselves, from Kermit Ruffins to Dr. John. Still one of the greatest shows ever made about American music.
